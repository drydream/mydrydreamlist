# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo user — the owner. Context: home ambient, often viewed embedded in a Home Assistant iframe on a wall panel or tablet. Not a public-facing tool; no accounts, no social layer. Opens to check what's currently being watched/read/played, bump episode or chapter progress, or add a new title. Also opens to browse and reminisce — scrolling through the archive is a use case, not a side effect.

## Product Purpose

A personal media tracker for anime, manga, series, and games. Single source of truth for what the user is watching, reading, and playing — progress numbers, status labels, and poster art. Success looks like: zero friction on the core loops (bump progress, add a title, see where things stand), and a space that feels genuinely worth opening.

## Brand Personality

Personal, focused, atmospheric.

The app is a private screening room, not a community tracker or streaming service. It should feel like a space the user inhabits rather than a tool they operate — intimate, deliberate, with the cover art as the visual anchor of every interaction.

## Anti-references

- **MAL / AniList** — cluttered, data-dense, forum-energy. Too much chrome, too many numbers, no atmosphere.
- **Letterboxd** — social-first diary format. Review-heavy, follow-count driven. Wrong register entirely.
- **Generic shadcn dark** — the default admin-panel dark theme: achromatic, could belong to any SaaS product, no point of view.
- **Netflix / streaming UI** — commercial carousels, hero banners, autoplay, marketing energy. The UI should never feel like it's selling something.

## Design Principles

1. **Content is the surface.** Poster art and cover images are not decoration — they define the visual field. UI elements are guests in that space. Dark backgrounds exist to make imagery sing, not to look like software.

2. **Private archive, not public tracker.** No social signals, no global rankings, no comparison UI. Nothing that implies an audience. Every screen should feel like opening a personal notebook.

3. **Atmosphere earns its place.** Every shadow, blur, gradient, and overlay must intensify the imagery or serve the task. Atmospheric choices that don't do useful work get cut.

4. **Function and feeling coexist.** Core interactions must be immediate — tapping progress, filtering, adding a title — and the space must feel inhabited. Neither sacrifices the other.

5. **No commercial signals.** Avoid all patterns that read as streaming service (hero banners, carousels, autoplay indicators) or tracker community (ratings, review counts, global scores). This is a personal index.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Single user, personal tool. No strict WCAG level required. Functional contrast and keyboard access are sufficient. No special accommodations needed beyond sensible defaults.
